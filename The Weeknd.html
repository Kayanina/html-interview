Trilogy

Montreal

Trilogy is an Album combining three Mixtapes the Weeknd released for free downloading in 2011: "House of Balloons", "Echoes of Silence" and "Thursday". The songs revolve around themes of love, lust, drugs and the dark side of fame.The lyrics often explore the complexities of relationships, personal struggles, and the hedonistic lifestyle, often intertwined with drug use and the consequences that come with it. "Trilogy" played a significant role in establishing him as a prominent figure in contemporary R&B and payved the way for his subsequent commercial success.

Kissland

Odd Look (ft. Kavinsky)

"Kissland is The Weeknd's debut studio album, released in 2013. The album marks a shift in The Weeknd's sound from the mixtapes included in "Trilogy." While the mixtapes were characterized by a more underground and experimental vibe, "Kiss Land" sees The Weeknd embracing a more polished and mainstream sound while still maintaining the dark and moody undertones that define his music and it showcases The Weeknd's ability to blend R&B, electronic, and atmospheric elements, contributing to his reputation as a genre-defying artist. The album received generally positive reviews for its production and The Weeknd's distinctive vocal style, solidifying his position as a major force in the music industry.
